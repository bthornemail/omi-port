# The Three Axes of Canonical Progression

## A First-Principles Reconstruction

---

# 1. The First Principle

Nothing begins with an object.

Everything begins with the absence of distinction.

In OMI, the canonical origin is therefore not an element, a variable, or a value.

It is the undistinguished boundary:

```text
NULL • NULL
```

This is the neutral observer.

No relation has yet been declared.

No interpretation has yet been accepted.

No projection has yet been rendered.

The system therefore begins not with data, but with the possibility of relation.

---

# 2. The First Distinction

The first operation is not computation.

It is separation.

A separator creates two regions that may later become related.

The ASCII control plane preserves this progression exactly:

```text
NULL

↓

FS

↓

GS

↓

RS

↓

US

↓

SPACE
```

The control codes do not merely separate text.

They construct progressively finer boundaries of interpretation.

Space (`0x20`) is therefore not "blank."

It is the first readable boundary.

Readable notation is earned only after the control hierarchy has established lawful structure.

---

# 3. Canonical Progression

Canonical progression is the deterministic movement from undifferentiated possibility toward structured relation.

This progression is independent of physical clocks.

It is logical.

A declaration advances because additional structure has become available, not because external time has elapsed.

The progression is:

```text

↓

↓

Relation

↓

Validation

↓

Receipt

↓

```

Every artifact in the system follows this ordering.

---

# 4. Three Independent Axes

Canonical progression unfolds along three independent axes.

These axes describe how structure develops.

They are not clocks.

---

The control hierarchy provides canonical position.


Position establishes scope.

Nothing about interpretation has yet occurred.

---

## Axis II — Inversion

Every declaration possesses a forward interpretation and an inverse interpretation.

These are represented by the canonical pair:

```text
OMI

↔

IMO
```

Neither side is primary.

Each determines the other.

Forward traversal constructs relation.

Inverse traversal verifies relation.

Meaning therefore exists through reversible correspondence rather than isolated symbols.

---

## Axis III — Chirality

Progression is not purely linear.

It possesses orientation.

The system therefore distinguishes between structural symmetry and ordered traversal.

This orientation is represented by deterministic chirality.

Operations such as circular inversion and snub truncation preserve orientation while permitting progression.

Rather than collapsing into mirrored duplication, the system preserves directional structure throughout every transformation.
